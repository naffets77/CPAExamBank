CPA Exam Structure

The CPA exam consists of four sections:

    Auditing and Attestation ( AUD ) Business Environment and Concepts ( BEC) Financial Accounting and Reporting ( FAR ) Regulation ( REG )

These four sections consist of multiple-choice questions. Sections AUD, FAR and REG consist of task-based simulation questions (TBS) as well. BEC contains a section for written communication questions but no task-based simulation questions.

CPA Question Types and Sections

1. Testlets (aka. Multiple choice questions) - The CPA exam is divided into 3 sections called testlets. A testlet consists of 10 to 25 questions. Candidates will be able to address and return to quesetion responses only within the current testlet. 80% of the CPA exam contains multiple choice questions. This is why we urge many of you to build a strong foundation using our practice questions. The CPA Exam Bank contains thousands of various multiple choice questions to keep you sharp and constantly challenged. Perfecting your strategy in answering multiple choice questions will increase your chances of passing the CPA exam.

2. Simulations (aka Case studies) - Accounting is tested in simulations, also known as Case studies. Simulations consist of a variety of tasks. These include: searching various database, utilizing spreadsheets/forms and completing written communication tasks. Simulations are utilized to measure your analysis, communication and research abilities.

Note: The BEC section does not contain simulations questions. All other sections will contain 1 or 2 simulation questions. Simulation questions will require 20 - 40 minutes.

CPA Exam Scoring

Scoring: CPA exam sections are reported on a scaled from 0 to 99. A total reported score of 75 must be achieved in order to pass each section. The total score for the following sections; AUD, FAR AND REG are a weighted combination of scaled scores from the MC and TBS questions. For the BEC section, the total score is a weighted combination from the MC and written communication tasks.


Remember, the total reported score which requires 75 to pass each section is NOT a percentage and should not be interpreted as a percentage.

CPA Exam Time Allocation

Time allotted: AUD: 4 hours Format: 3 multiple choice testlets and 7 short task-based simulation questions BEC - 3 hours Format: 3 multiple choice testlets and 3 written simulations FAR - 4 hours Format: 3 multiple choice testlets and 7 short task-based simulations REG - 3 hours Format: 3 multiple choice testlets and 6 short task-based simulations
